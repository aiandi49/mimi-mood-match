A MOOD-MATCHING LISTENING COMPANION · CONCEPT HUB № 21

Mimi's Mood Match

Tell it how you feel, and it hands you the song from one catalog that fits — mood tagging, a personal library, and streaks built around a single artist's discography.

Built by AI&I

Mimi's Mood Match is a real, fully built full-stack application — a mood-based song matching tool built entirely around Mariah Carey's discography. A user describes their emotional tone and energy level, and the app matches them to a track tagged for that mood, from Melancholic and Nostalgic to Empowered and Festive.

What makes it more than a playlist generator is the depth underneath: a manually mood-tagged song library, a matching algorithm that scores tracks against emotional tone and energy level, a personal listening library, a streak and achievement system to encourage daily use, and an admin tool for managing the catalog.

Worth flagging up front: this concept is built entirely on one real artist's copyrighted catalog and name. It's documented here as a concept and personal project — any commercial version would need licensing from the relevant rights holders before it could be sold or distributed. The five W's below reflect that.

What follows is the same structure AI&I uses for every concept trial: the five W's of the idea, the five W's of monetizing it, and the developer information behind it — all in one place.

W

The First W

Who: The Listener Who Wants a Song, Not a Shuffle

Mimi's Mood Match is built for people who already love Mariah Carey's catalog and want a faster way into the right track for how they feel right now, instead of scrolling a 20-plus-track discography hoping something lands.

It's a mood-based listening companion, not a clinical tool — it's designed to make finding the right song easier, not to diagnose, treat, or replace care for anxiety, depression, or any other condition.

"The catalog already runs the emotional range — melancholic, nostalgic, empowered, festive. The app's whole job is getting a listener to the right one faster."

The Second W

What: Four Systems, One Catalog From Mood to Habit

The app follows a listener from a mood check-in to a returning habit across four connected systems.

System 01

Feel

A mood input flow capturing emotional tone and energy level, the starting point for every match.

System 02

Match

A scoring algorithm pairing mood tags — Empowered, Melancholic, Nostalgic, Romantic, Festive, Sassy, Hopeful, Dreamy — against the user's input to return the best-fit track.

System 03

Track

A personal library and results history, so a listener can revisit past matches and see their own emotional listening pattern over time.

System 04

Grow

A streak and achievement system rewarding daily check-ins, plus an admin tool for managing and tagging the underlying catalog.

The Third & Fourth W

When & Where: The Moment Before You Know What to Play

When it's used: right at the moment someone knows how they feel but hasn't decided what to listen to — a quick mood check-in stands in for scrolling.

Where it lives: as a companion app for an existing fan, layered on top of however they already listen — the app matches and points to a track, it isn't a streaming service on its own.

"Why build mood-matching around one artist instead of a whole streaming catalog? Because a deep, well-tagged discography from one voice is a more coherent emotional map than a shallow tag across a million songs."

The Five W's — Monetization

Who We'd Sell To, and What Would Actually Be For Sale

Who we'd sell to: fans of this specific catalog, or — reframed as a template — any artist's estate, label, or fan-engagement team wanting a mood-matching companion app for their own catalog.

What's for sale: not the catalog itself — that isn't ours to sell — but the mood-tagging engine, the matching algorithm, and the streak and library system as a reusable app template that could be re-skinned for a properly licensed artist catalog.

When We'd Sell

Once there's a licensing conversation in place with the actual rights holders — the catalog owner, publisher, or artist's team — since the underlying songs and name aren't ours to commercialize as-is.

Where We'd Sell

Directly to a label, artist estate, or fan-engagement team as a white-label mood-matching product, rather than distributing this specific build commercially without a license.

"Why frame the pitch around the engine, not the catalog? Because the mood-tagging and matching logic is the reusable, sellable part — the songs and name are someone else's IP, and any real pitch has to say so upfront."

A Note on How This Was Built

Read From the Real Codebase, With One Honest Caveat

  • Every mechanic here — the mood-tagging system, the scoring algorithm, the streak and achievement logic — comes directly from the actual application, not invented for this hub.
  • The four-system framing — Feel, Match, Track, Grow — is AI&I's own way of organizing what the app already does.
  • The catalog itself is Mariah Carey's copyrighted work, and the name and likeness used throughout are hers. This hub doesn't reproduce any lyrics, and treats the concept as a personal project and technical case study rather than a ready-to-launch commercial product.
  • Any path to real monetization runs through licensing with the actual rights holders first — that's a genuine constraint, not a footnote.
